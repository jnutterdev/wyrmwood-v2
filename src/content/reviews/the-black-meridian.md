The *Black Meridian* is 96 pages that feel like 400. It achieves this through relentless density — every hex has a secret, every location carries weather, every NPC has a reason to lie to the party.

This is old-school design philosophy executed with modern layout sensibility. The tables are usable at the table. The art is evocative rather than decorative. And the central mystery — the meridian itself, a line drawn across the map by an extinct civilisation — is the kind of hook that generates years of campaign play.
