Epic metal has a long memory. *Iron Hymnal, Volume II* proves that the best artists in the genre are still finding new rooms in the fortress.

The album's opening salvo — three tracks of unbroken momentum — announces intentions immediately. This is not an album of subtlety or quiet moments. It is the sound of something large moving through the world.

"Stormwall" remains the standout: eight minutes of layered choirs, a guitar tone like a siege engine, and a chorus that sounds like it was written to be sung by armies. It is, in the best possible sense, absurd.
