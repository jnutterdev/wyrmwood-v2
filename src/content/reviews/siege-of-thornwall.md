*Siege of Thornwall* arrived marketed as a standalone wargame. It is that, and more — the 40-page setting document bundled in the box is quietly one of the best pieces of world-building released in any format this year.

The siege mechanics themselves are clean and brutal. Walls have hit points. Commanders have morale thresholds. The rules model exhaustion in a way that most tactical games ignore at their peril.
