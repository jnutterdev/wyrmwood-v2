There are adventures that work despite their premise and adventures that work because of it. *The Tournament at Raven's Gate* is definitively the latter.

The setup is elegant: the players are competitors in a chivalric tournament, but the tournament is a cover for something much stranger. The two layers — the public performance of nobility and the private reality of whatever is happening in the stables at midnight — create a natural tension that the GM barely needs to manage.
